<DESCRIPTION>
The current bottleneck is the severe PD class (class 2) with F1=0.52. The confusion matrix shows 56 normal→severe and 34 mild→severe misclassifications, indicating the classifier struggles to distinguish severe from milder forms. 

Jerk (3rd derivative of rotation) captures movement smoothness and is a key biomarker in severe PD where movements become fragmented and dyskinetic. Adding jerk statistics as features will provide discriminative power for the severe class.

We extend the feature extraction to include:
1. Jerk (3rd derivative) statistics: mean, std, and abs_mean of acceleration differences
2. These features are added in a new section after acceleration statistics
3. The features are computed only when T>3 to ensure valid derivatives

This change targets the primary bottleneck by adding neurologically meaningful features that quantify the loss of movement smoothness in advanced PD.
