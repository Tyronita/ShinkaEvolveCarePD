<DESCRIPTION>
Jerk (the third derivative of position) is a sensitive biomechanical marker for movement disorders like Parkinson's Disease, capturing the characteristic "start-stop" or "tremulous" gait patterns in severe cases. The current feature set includes velocity and acceleration but stops at the second derivative. Adding jerk features specifically for the pelvis, hips, and ankles (key joints affected in PD) will provide the classifier with more discriminative signals for the severe class. This change directly addresses the bottleneck where 56+34=90 severe cases are misclassified (mostly as normal), as jerkiness is a hallmark of advanced PD gait. The implementation computes the absolute mean and std of jerk for these joints, appending 108 new dimensions to the feature vector. This is a targeted, low-risk enhancement grounded in clinical gait analysis.
</DESCRIPTION>
