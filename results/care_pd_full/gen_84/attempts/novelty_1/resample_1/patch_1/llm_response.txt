<DESCRIPTION>
The current FFT-based gait rhythm features are computed on the entire sequence, which averages out within-walk temporal variations such as gait initiation instability, freezing-of-gait episodes, or cadence deterioration—phenomena more prevalent in moderate-to-severe PD (class 2). To better capture these dynamics, I propose extending the FFT feature extraction to operate on 4 non-overlapping temporal segments of the walk. This will allow the model to detect rhythm irregularities that are masked in whole-sequence FFT.

Specifically, for pelvis (joint 0), left ankle (7), and right ankle (8), we compute FFT on each of the 4 segments for the first 3 rotation components (6D → 3 used). From each segment, we extract:
- Dominant frequency in gait band (0.5–3.0 Hz)
- Gait-band power ratio
- High-frequency power ratio (>3.0 Hz)

This yields 3 joints × 3 features × 4 segments = 36 dimensions. Additionally, we compute the std and linear trend across segments (3 joints × 3 features × 2 stats = 18 dims), capturing inter-segment variability and progression. Total new features: 54.

These features directly target the poor performance in folds 1, 2, 5, and 6 (macro_F1 ≤ 0.48) where subject-level gait rhythm instability is a key confounder. By modeling stride-level cadence dynamics, the classifier gains discriminative power at the mild/severe boundary, where freezing and arrhythmia are clinical hallmarks.
